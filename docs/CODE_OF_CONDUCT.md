<h1 align="center">Code of Conduct</h1>

**The Lupaxa Project** is committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and respectful community for everyone.

We believe that successful open-source communities are built on collaboration, professionalism, and mutual respect. Every interaction, whether in code,
documentation, discussions, issue trackers, or other community spaces, should reflect these values.

This Code of Conduct applies across all repositories, discussions, and community spaces that form part of **The Lupaxa Project**.

## Our Standards

We expect everyone participating in **The Lupaxa Project** to contribute to a positive and inclusive environment.

Examples of positive behaviour include:

- Being respectful and considerate of differing opinions, experiences, and perspectives.
- Providing constructive feedback in a professional manner.
- Accepting feedback gracefully and being willing to learn.
- Focusing discussions on improving the project.
- Recognising and appreciating the time and effort contributed by others.
- Helping create an environment where people feel welcome to participate.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Personal attacks, insults, or deliberately inflammatory comments.
- Trolling or intentionally disrupting discussions.
- The use of sexualised language or imagery.
- Publishing another person's private or confidential information without their explicit permission.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or collaborative community.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for helping ensure that community discussions remain constructive, welcoming, and aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Where necessary, maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, discussions, pull requests, or other contributions that violate these standards.

Responses will always aim to be fair, proportionate, and focused on maintaining a healthy community.

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please let us know.

Reports may be submitted by contacting the project team at: **[conduct@thelupaxaproject.org](mailto:conduct@thelupaxaproject.org)**

All reports will be treated seriously and handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. We will investigate concerns promptly, fairly, and with respect
for everyone involved.

We understand that reporting unacceptable behaviour can be difficult and appreciate those who help us maintain a safe and welcoming community.

## Enforcement

When a violation of this Code of Conduct is confirmed, maintainers may take one or more of the following actions, depending on the severity, frequency, and
context of the behaviour:

1. **Private Reminder**
   A private conversation or written reminder explaining the concern and the expected standard of behaviour.

2. **Official Warning**
   A formal warning advising that continued behaviour may result in further action.

3. **Temporary Restrictions**
   Temporary suspension from discussions, issue trackers, pull requests, or other community spaces.

4. **Permanent Removal**
   Permanent removal from one or more repositories or community spaces where continued participation is no longer appropriate.

Enforcement decisions are intended to protect the community, not to punish individuals. Every effort will be made to apply this Code of Conduct consistently,
fairly, and proportionately.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community interactions associated with **The Lupaxa Project**, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues
- Pull Requests
- Discussions
- Documentation
- Project websites
- Community communication channels
- Events or activities conducted under The Lupaxa Project name

Behaviour outside these spaces may also be considered where it has a direct impact on the safety, wellbeing, or integrity of the project or its community.

## Acknowledgements

This Code of Conduct is inspired by widely adopted open-source community standards and has been adapted to reflect the values, culture, and governance of
**The Lupaxa Project**.
