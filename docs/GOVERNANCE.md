<h1 align="center">Governance</h1>

This document defines how **The Lupaxa Project** is governed and how decisions are made across the project.

Its purpose is to provide a clear, transparent, and lightweight governance model that encourages community participation while ensuring the long-term
consistency, stability, and sustainability of the ecosystem.

## Governing Principles

The governance of **The Lupaxa Project** is founded on a small number of core principles:

- Openness wherever practical.
- Transparent decision making.
- Respectful collaboration.
- Technical excellence.
- Security by design.
- Pragmatic leadership.

We believe that the best decisions are reached through open discussion and technical merit, while recognising that clear leadership is sometimes necessary
to maintain direction and consistency.

## Roles

### Project Steward

The Project Steward provides overall direction for **The Lupaxa Project**.

Responsibilities include:

- Defining the long-term vision of the project.
- Maintaining consistency across organisations and repositories.
- Resolving governance questions.
- Making final decisions where consensus cannot be reached.
- Approving significant organisational or cross-project changes.

The Project Steward aims to guide rather than control, encouraging discussion and community participation wherever possible.

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the day-to-day stewardship of individual repositories.

Their responsibilities are described in detail in the accompanying **Maintainers** document and typically include:

- Reviewing contributions.
- Managing releases.
- Maintaining documentation.
- Triaging issues and pull requests.
- Ensuring repository quality and security.

### Contributors

Contributors are the foundation of **The Lupaxa Project**.

Anyone may contribute by:

- Reporting bugs.
- Suggesting improvements.
- Improving documentation.
- Contributing code.
- Reviewing pull requests.
- Participating in discussions.

Contributions are evaluated on their quality, relevance, and technical merit rather than the contributor's background or level of experience.

## Decision Making

The Lupaxa Project favours discussion and consensus wherever practical.

Most decisions are made by the maintainers responsible for the affected repository. Contributors are encouraged to participate in discussions, provide
feedback, and help shape the direction of the project.

### Routine Changes

Routine work, such as bug fixes, documentation improvements, dependency updates, and minor enhancements, generally follows a straightforward process:

1. A contributor opens a Pull Request.
2. One or more maintainers review the proposed changes.
3. Automated quality checks complete successfully.
4. The Pull Request is approved and merged.

### Significant Changes

Changes that substantially affect behaviour, architecture, compatibility, or multiple repositories should normally begin with a discussion before
implementation.

Examples include:

- New major features.
- Breaking changes.
- Architectural redesigns.
- Organisation-wide standards.
- Cross-repository initiatives.

Where possible, consensus should be reached through open discussion. If consensus cannot be achieved, the Project Steward will make the final decision after
considering the technical merits and community feedback.

## Repository Independence

Although repositories share common standards and governance, each repository remains responsible for its own:

- Release schedule.
- Development priorities.
- Technology choices.
- Roadmap.
- Day-to-day maintenance.

This allows repositories to evolve independently while remaining part of the wider Lupaxa ecosystem.

## Maintainers

Maintainers are entrusted with the stewardship of individual repositories.

Their responsibilities include:

- Reviewing contributions.
- Maintaining documentation.
- Managing releases.
- Ensuring repository quality.
- Encouraging constructive community participation.

The expectations for maintainers are described in the accompanying **Maintainers** document.

## Amendments

This governance document will evolve as **The Lupaxa Project** grows.

Proposed changes should be made through the normal Pull Request process and discussed openly before adoption.

Changes that significantly affect governance should be approved by the Project Steward.

## Questions

Questions relating to governance, project structure, or organisational decisions are welcome through GitHub Discussions or the appropriate repository issue
tracker.
