<h1 align="center">Maintainers</h1>

This document defines the role, responsibilities, and expectations of maintainers across **The Lupaxa Project**.

Maintainers play an essential role in ensuring repositories remain welcoming, well-maintained, secure, and technically robust. They act as stewards of their
repositories while supporting contributors and helping projects evolve over time.

The Project Steward provides overall direction for **The Lupaxa Project** and acts as the final point of escalation for governance matters where consensus
cannot be reached.

### Repository Maintainers

Repository maintainers are responsible for the day-to-day stewardship of one or more repositories.

As The Lupaxa Project continues to grow, additional maintainers may be appointed based on sustained, high-quality contributions and a demonstrated commitment
to the project's values and standards.

## Responsibilities

Maintainers are expected to:

- Review Pull Requests fairly and constructively.
- Encourage and support contributors.
- Maintain a welcoming and respectful community.
- Ensure documentation remains accurate and up to date.
- Manage releases and repository administration.
- Triage issues and discussions.
- Promote secure development practices.
- Maintain the overall quality and long-term health of their repositories.

While maintainers are responsible for guiding projects, they are encouraged to make decisions openly and collaboratively wherever possible.

## Expectations

Maintainers should strive to:

- Lead by example.
- Communicate respectfully and professionally.
- Be transparent when making decisions.
- Provide constructive feedback.
- Apply project standards consistently.
- Balance stability with innovation.
- Encourage community participation.

Maintainers are trusted to exercise sound judgement while recognising that no individual has all the answers. Healthy discussion and differing opinions are
an important part of open-source development.

## Becoming a Maintainer

Maintainers are appointed based on demonstrated commitment to **The Lupaxa Project** rather than through a formal application process.

When considering new maintainers, we typically look for contributors who consistently demonstrate:

- High-quality technical contributions.
- Constructive participation in discussions and code reviews.
- A willingness to help other contributors.
- Good judgement and professionalism.
- Respect for the project's values and standards.
- An interest in the long-term success of the repository.

Appointment as a maintainer is recognition of sustained contribution and trust within the community.

## Stepping Down

Maintainers may step down from their role at any time.

Where practical, we encourage maintainers to:

- Notify the Project Steward or other maintainers.
- Complete or hand over any active work.
- Assist with a smooth transition of responsibilities.

Maintainers may also be removed where appropriate, for example due to prolonged inactivity, at their own request, or where continued maintainership is no
longer in the best interests of the project.

## Decision Making

Maintainers are encouraged to make routine repository decisions independently while keeping discussions open and transparent.

For significant technical or organisational changes, maintainers should seek consensus wherever practical and involve the wider community where appropriate.

Where consensus cannot be reached, governance follows the process described in the accompanying **Governance** document.

## Supporting the Community

Maintainers play an important role in shaping the community as well as the software.

This includes:

- Welcoming new contributors.
- Encouraging respectful discussion.
- Helping contributors understand project standards.
- Recognising valuable community contributions.
- Promoting an inclusive and collaborative environment.

Technical excellence and community health are equally important to the long-term success of **The Lupaxa Project**.
